Step 3 of 4
How should captions look?
This controls what language and script your caption text appears in. Every line in the SRT file follows this format.
Phonetic
Every spoken word is written in Roman (English) letters exactly as it sounds. Best for audiences who understand the language but can't read the native script.
"నమస్తే అందరికీ" → "Namaste andariki"
Native
Output in the original language script with proper formatting. Everything stays in native characters. Best for formal content and native script readers.
"Hello everyone" → "హలో అందరికీ"
Tenglish
Telugu words stay in Telugu script. English words stay in English. This is exactly how mixed-language speech looks when written naturally — the most authentic output for code-switched audio.
"నేను recently ఒక interview attend చేశాను"
English
Full translation into English. The AI understands meaning and rewrites every caption naturally in English. Best for English-only audiences or global reach.
"నేను చాలా కష్టపడ్డాను" → "I worked very hard"

How to use SAYCAP

SAYCAP converts your audio files into perfectly timed SRT caption files. These SRT files can be directly imported into Adobe Premiere Pro, CapCut, or any video editor — they will automatically sync with your video timeline.

Upload MP3 or M4A

Export audio from your video editor and upload it here. Files up to 500MB are supported. Long files are automatically split into chunks.

Tip: Export at 128kbps MP3 for smallest file size

Choose output mode

Phonetic gives Roman script. Native gives original script. Tenglish/Hinglish gives mixed. English translates everything to English.

For most videos: Phonetic or Tenglish

Import SRT in Premiere

In Adobe Premiere Pro: File → Import → select your .srt file. Drag it onto your timeline above the video track. Captions sync automatically.

Works in Premiere Pro, CapCut, DaVinci Resolve

3 words per caption

SAYCAP automatically groups captions into 3-word blocks. This matches the rhythm of how people read subtitles and is the industry standard for short-form video.

Optimized for Reels, Shorts, YouTube

Pro tip: For Telugu content with English mixed in, use Tenglish mode. It keeps Telugu words in Telugu script and English words in English — exactly how your audience naturally reads mixed-language content.
